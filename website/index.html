01 — The bud

Eight gestures. One target.

Everything Bloom does starts at a 44 pixel bud that floats above your windows at 85% opacity. It never steals focus, and it stays exactly where you put it.

Click

Open the dial

The ring blooms outward, centered on the bud, wherever it happens to be sitting.

Double-click

Dictate

Starts voice dictation by default — or fires your starred favourite instead, your call.

Long-press

Read aloud

Reads your current text selection by default — or a second, separate favourite.

Drag

Move it anywhere

Across any monitor. It edge-snaps to the nearest border and remembers where it landed.

Scroll

Cycle pinned

A small chip cycles your pinned actions — click the bud to run whichever one is showing.

Middle-click

Hide to tray

The bud vanishes. Hotkeys keep working; the tray icon brings it back.

Right-click

Quick menu

Edit Actions · Settings · Pin position · Hide · Quit. No hunting through a preferences maze.

Drop a file

Three-node ring

Drag any file onto the bud: Open · Open containing folder · Copy path.

02 — The dial

Rings on rings. Nothing is ever replaced.

Opening a folder doesn't swap the view out from under you. Its contents appear in a new ring just outside — and the ring you came from shrinks back and dims to 35%, except for the one node on the path that got you there.

  1. 1

    Click the bud

  2. 2

    Open a folder

    Contents bloom into an outer ring. The layer behind dims but stays on screen.

  3. 3

    App, URL, terminal, toggle, script, macro — whatever you put there.

  4. 4

Fully keyboard operable — the dial never needs a mouse.

03 — Voice, entirely local

Speak it. Read it. No internet.

The Whisper model ships inside the installer and runs on your CPU. Nothing is uploaded, no key to paste, no account to make. Works on a plane.

Speech → TextCtrlAltD

Voice dictation

Bloom captures your mic, transcribes with an embedded Xenova/whisper-base.en model, and types the result straight at your cursor — any app, any text field.

  • Long-form safe — 30s chunks with 5s stride, so a two-minute ramble transcribes whole
  • Live level meter on the bud while the mic is hot
Text → SpeechCtrlAltR

Read-aloud

Select text anywhere and Bloom reads it back through your OS's own speech engine — no model download, no latency, the voices you already have installed.

WindowsSystem.Speech Linuxspd-say / espeak-ng macOSsay
  • Pick any installed system voice, tune the rate
  • Fires again to stop mid-sentence

Ctrl+Alt+D and Ctrl+Alt+R are always live — completely independent of whatever you've pointed the bud's double-click and long-press at.

If it can be triggered, Bloom can bloom it.

Every node in the tree is either a folder or one of these ten. Each one reports back with a toast.

01

A searchable picker over everything installed.

02

One link, or a whole tab group at once. Target a specific browser and profile.

03

Working directory plus an auto-run command. gnome-terminal, konsole, xfce4-terminal, or your default.

04

Seventeen of them — see below. Destructive ones can be flagged to ask first.

05

Media Controls

Play/pause, next, previous, volume up and down, straight through the OS media keys.

06

Canned text, either copied to the clipboard or typed at the cursor. Email, signature, that one shell incantation.

07

Jump straight to a path in the system file manager or default app.

08

05 — Global hotkeys

Never touch the bud at all.

Four global bindings are always registered, even with the bud hidden in the tray. Every one of them is rebindable.

CtrlAltSpace

Summon the dial

Opens the ring on whichever display your cursor is on — bud visible or not.

CtrlShiftSpace

Command palette

Fuzzy-search every action in the tree by name and fire it. No ring navigation at all.

CtrlAltD

Toggle dictation

Mic on, mic off. Text lands at the cursor when you stop.

CtrlAltR

Read selection

Speaks whatever is highlighted. Press again to cut it off.

Quickfire

Any node can have its own hotkey.

Beyond the four globals, bind a dedicated accelerator to any single action in your tree. Your terminal on Ctrl+Alt+T, your work tabs on Ctrl+Alt+W — straight to the thing, no dial in between.

CtrlAltT→Terminal · Projects
CtrlAltW→Browser · Work Tabs
CtrlAltS→System · Screenshot

06 — Settings

Six tabs. Then get out of the way.

A proper resizable window — not the transient dial — for the rare moments you actually need to edit something.

Your whole tree, searchable.

An outline of every folder and action. Drag to reorder or reparent, star a favourite, pin the ones you want on the scroll chip, and hit Run now to test any node before it ever goes live on the dial.

One look, tuned to taste.

Bloom keeps one considered visual identity. What you tune is size, node spacing, ring radius and gap, motion pacing and the accent colour, with a live preview beside the sliders.

Rebind everything.

All four global hotkeys, plus per-node quickfire bindings. Choose what double-click does (dictate or your favourite) and what long-press does (read aloud or a second favourite), independently. Tune hover-to-open delay, edge snapping and scroll-cycling.

Whole setups, swapped instantly.

Save your tree, favourites, pinned actions and appearance as a named profile, then switch between them. Work, gaming, presentation — a different dial for each context, one click apart.

Startup and behaviour.

Launch at login (on by default the first run, off in one click), tray behaviour, bud size and idle opacity, pin-in-place, and the reset-onboarding button if you want the intro tour again.

Updates on your terms.

Version, licence, and an updater that checks but never downloads behind your back — you approve every update, with a live progress readout. Pick the stable or beta channel, or turn auto-checking off entirely.

07 — Under it all

The boring parts, done properly.

Your config cannot eat itself

One JSON file, written atomically via a temp file and rename — a crash mid-save can never leave a half-written config. Every save rolls the previous version into a backup, five deep. If the live file is ever corrupt, Bloom walks the backups in order and boots from the newest one that parses, then tells you which one it recovered from.

Every monitor, one space

The overlay spans the union of all your displays, so ring coordinates are display-independent. Plug in a screen, unplug it, change resolution mid-session — Bloom re-lays itself out.

Click-through when idle

When no ring is open, the overlay ignores your mouse entirely. Toasts and the scroll chip show without ever intercepting a click meant for the window underneath.

Never steals focus

The bud and the dial float above everything without taking focus from what you're typing in. Dictation types into the app you were already using.

Honest failures

An action that fails says so, by name and reason, in a toast. No silent no-ops, no spinner that never resolves.

Starts with you

Launch-at-login is enabled once on first run and is yours to turn off. A tray icon is always there to bring the bud back from hiding.

Nothing phones home

No telemetry, no account, no cloud. The only network call Bloom ever makes is the update check you can switch off.
